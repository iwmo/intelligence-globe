<task type="checkpoint:human-verify" gate="blocking">
  <what-built>
    Three camera navigation features:
    1. Double-click zoom toward cursor (NAV-01) — GlobeView.tsx LEFT_DOUBLE_CLICK handler
    2. Tilt/pitch widget with TOP / 45° / HRZ presets (NAV-02) — CameraControlWidget.tsx
    3. On-screen +/− zoom buttons (NAV-03) — CameraControlWidget.tsx
  </what-built>
  <how-to-verify>
Open the app at http://localhost:5173 and run through this checklist:

**NAV-01 — Double-click zoom:**
1. Double-click on any ocean or land area — camera should smoothly fly toward the clicked point (not screen center). Repeat 3-4 times zooming progressively closer.
2. Double-click on an empty area of sky (above the horizon, no globe visible) — camera should NOT move.
3. Double-click on a visible aircraft or ship icon — camera should zoom toward the globe beneath the entity. The detail panel for that entity should NOT open.
4. Single-click on any entity — the detail panel SHOULD still open (verify the 200ms debounce is imperceptible, not blocking normal clicks).

**NAV-02 — Tilt widget:**
5. Locate the CameraControlWidget in the bottom-right corner. It should be above the BottomStatusBar and not overlap CesiumJS credit attribution.
6. Click "TOP" — globe should reorient to a straight-down bird's-eye view.
7. Click "45°" — globe should tilt to 45° oblique angle.
8. Click "HRZ" — globe should tilt to near-horizon (low pitch, landscape view).
9. Click tilt buttons mid-flight (e.g., during a double-click zoom) — should NOT cause camera stutter.

**NAV-03 — Zoom buttons:**
10. Click "+" several times — camera should zoom in incrementally.
11. Click "−" several times — camera should zoom out incrementally.
12. Confirm the zoom step feels deliberate (larger per-click than scroll wheel, which is the intended 0.3 factor vs 0.12).

**Layout:**
13. Confirm CameraControlWidget does not overlap: CesiumJS credits (bottom-right corner), BottomStatusBar, or the MGRS/telemetry block in the right column.
  </how-to-verify>
